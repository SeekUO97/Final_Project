    ‚Ä¢ Be ready to simultaneously release the front brake with your right
      hand and begin engaging the clutch with your left hand.
    ‚Ä¢ Stay balanced and maintain full control of the bike as you prepare to move forward.

  5. Ready.. Set.. Go!
    ‚Ä¢Slowly release the clutch lever with your left hand until you reach the friction zone.
      ‚ö†Ô∏è This is where the bike will begin to pull forward.
    ‚Ä¢ As the clutch engages, gradually roll on a small amount of throttle (if needed) to maintain engine stability.

  6. Maintaining Speed and Control:
    ‚Ä¢ Once the bike is moving, maintain a consistent speed using light, controlled throttle.
    ‚Ä¢ Keep your body relaxed and ensure your grip on the handlebars is firm but not tense.
    ‚Ä¢ Scan the road ahead and stay aware of your surroundings.
    ‚Ä¢ Keep your feet on the foot pegs and your eyes looking where you want to go

  7. Smooth and Controlled Shifting:
    ‚Ä¢ As your speed increases or decreases and engine RPM rises and falls, prepare to shift accordingly.
    ‚Ä¢ Fully disengage the clutch by pulling in the clutch lever with your left hand.
    ‚Ä¢ Gradually roll off the throttle.
    ‚Ä¢ Use your left foot to shift the gear shift lever, lift it up to upshift or down to downshift.
    ‚Ä¢ Release the clutch smoothly while gradually rolling back on the throttle to maintain power and balance.
    ‚Ä¢ Avoid abrupt gear changes to reduce wear on the transmission and clutch.
    ‚Ä¢ Maintain a relaxed posture and scan ahead for potential hazards during the shift.

  8. Executing Safe and Controlled Turns:
    1. Slow down before the turn by gradually rolling off the throttle.
    2. Apply brakes gently as needed, using approximately 70% front brake and 30% rear brake pressure,
        avoid braking during the turn itself.
    3. As you are entering the turn, look through the turn and focus on your intended path, not directly in front of the bike.
    4. Lean your body and the motorcycle smoothly into the turn, maintaining a steady throttle.
    5. Keep a firm, relaxed grip on the handlebars and avoid sudden movements to maintain control.
    6. As you exit the turn, gently accelerate to regain speed and stabilize the bike.

  9. Wrapping it up!
    1. Gradually reduce your speed by rolling off the throttle and applying both brakes.
    2. Come to a complete stop and shift the bike into neutral.
    3. Deploy the kickstand firmly, ensure there is nothing underneath it, and confirm the bike is stable.
    4. Turn the kill switch to the OFF position, and turn the ignition off and remove the key.
    5. Carefully dismount the bike by swinging your right leg over the seat and stepping down on the left side of the motorcycle.
